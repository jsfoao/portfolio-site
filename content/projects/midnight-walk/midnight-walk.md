## Overview

I contributed to the development of *The Midnight Walk*, which released across multiple platforms, including PS5, PSVR2, and PC (Steam and SteamVR). For the past few months, I've also assisting with development on the Nintendo Switch 2 port in parallel with my other responsabilities in the company.

I worked extensively across both the VR and flatscreen experiences. As Lead VR Programmer, I was responsible for the development of core VR gameplay systems and features. This included physics-based hand interactions driven by a custom PID controller, as well as a hybrid player controller supporting comfort features, multiple locomotion modes (room-scale and virtual) and handling the many edge cases that a VR player controller brings.

In addition to my VR responsibilities, I played a key role in building foundational systems used across the entire game. I designed and developed core gameplay frameworks, including the interaction system, puzzle systems, and a hybrid UI system that supports both flatscreen and VR.

Working within a small team required strong ownership and adaptability. I was responsible not only for implementation but also for designing robust, reusable systems that could support multiple platforms. I also ensured that features consistently met performance and quality targets across all supported platforms.

## Highlights

- Successfully shipped a hybrid game from start to finish
- Owned development of the VR experience across PSVR2 and PCVR  
- Designed and implemented physics-based VR hand interactions using a custom PID controller  
- Built a hybrid player controller supporting:
  - Comfort settings  
  - Room-scale and artificial locomotion  
  - Head clipping and edge-case handling  
- Designed and developed core gameplay systems, including interaction and puzzle frameworks  
- Architected and implemented a hybrid UI system for both VR and flatscreen  
- Contributed to multi-platform development, including support for a Nintendo Switch 2 port  
- Ensured features met performance, quality targets and certification requirements across all platforms
